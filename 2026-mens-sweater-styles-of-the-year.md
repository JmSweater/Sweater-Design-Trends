## 📐 The Pattern Math: 1:0.8 Silhouette

Most traditional knitwear follows the human body too literally. If a wearer's natural shape is $1:1$ (straight up and down), a standard sweater amplifies that lack of structure. 

This project implements a bespoke tailoring ratio directly into industrial knit programming, targeting a **$1:0.8$ shoulder-to-waist proportion**.

### Optical & Mechanical Vectors
* **Vertical Rib Architecture:** Deep vertical ribs act as visual vectors, drawing the eye downward to create elongation.
* **Micro-Compression Side Panels:** Utilizes a high-tension micro-compression stitch that gently reins in the midsection without the restrictive elasticity of traditional activewear.
* **The Mid-Chest $V$-Line:** When the two-way zipper is tensioned to mid-chest, the resulting $V$-line frames the neck and jawline, replicating the structural optics of a bespoke blazer.

## 🧵 Production & Machinery

We have deprecated traditional cut-and-sew methods (front panel, back panel, sleeves stitched together) due to seam irritation and drape interruption.

* **Hardware Stack:** 110+ German **STOLL** flatbed machines.
* **Architecture:** Whole-garment programming. One continuous yarn enters, one complete jacket exits. 
* **Seam Count:** `0` (No shoulder seams, no side seams, no abrasive neck labels).
* **Material Blend:** Washable Merino Wool + GRS-Certified Bio-Based Fibers (Engineered for the "Monday Morning Test" — machine washable, zero pilling, zero shrinkage, instant wearability straight off the hanger).
